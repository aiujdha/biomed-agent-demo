# Clinical Trial Summary: ADC-101

## Trial Information

- **Trial ID:** NCT-ADC-101-2024
- **Title:** A Phase II Study of ADC-101 in Patients with HER2-Positive Advanced Solid Tumors
- **Sponsor:** BioMed Pharmaceuticals, Inc.
- **Status:** Recruiting

## Study Design

This is a single-arm, open-label, multicenter Phase II trial evaluating the efficacy and safety of ADC-101 in adult patients with HER2-positive (IHC 3+ or IHC 2+/FISH-positive) advanced solid tumors who have progressed on at least two prior lines of systemic therapy.

## Key Eligibility Criteria

### Inclusion Criteria
- Histologically confirmed advanced solid tumor with HER2 positivity.
- Age ≥ 18 years.
- ECOG performance status 0 or 1.
- At least one measurable lesion per RECIST v1.1.
- Adequate bone marrow, hepatic, and renal function.
- Life expectancy of at least 12 weeks.

### Exclusion Criteria
- Prior treatment with any ADC targeting HER2.
- History of interstitial lung disease or non-infectious pneumonitis.
- Clinically significant cardiac disease (LVEF < 50%).
- Active brain metastases requiring treatment.
- Uncontrolled infection or known HIV/hepatitis B or C infection.

## Treatment Regimen

ADC-101 is administered at 5.4 mg/kg via intravenous infusion every 3 weeks (21-day cycle). Premedication with antihistamines and antipyretics is recommended for the first infusion. Treatment continues until disease progression, unacceptable toxicity, or patient withdrawal.

## Endpoints

- **Primary Endpoint:** Objective response rate (ORR) per RECIST v1.1, assessed by independent review.
- **Secondary Endpoints:** Duration of response (DoR), progression-free survival (PFS), overall survival (OS), disease control rate (DCR), and safety profile.
- **Exploratory Endpoints:** Biomarker analysis including HER2 expression levels, ADC-101 pharmacokinetics, and anti-drug antibody formation.

## Sample Size

Approximately 120 patients will be enrolled across 20 sites in North America, Europe, and Asia.

## Statistical Considerations

The study uses a Simon two-stage optimal design. In stage 1, 28 patients will be enrolled. If at least 8 responses are observed, enrollment expands to a total of 120 patients. The null hypothesis ORR of ≤ 25% will be tested against the alternative of ≥ 40%, with 90% power at a one-sided alpha of 0.05.

## Safety Monitoring

An independent Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) will review safety data after every 20 patients complete at least one cycle of treatment. Dose modifications are specified for hematologic, pulmonary, and hepatic toxicities.
