# Cell Culture Standard Operating Procedure

## 1. Purpose

This SOP describes the standard procedure for culturing adherent mammalian cells (e.g., HEK293, HeLa) in a biosafety level 2 (BSL-2) laboratory.

## 2. Materials

- Complete growth medium: DMEM supplemented with 10% fetal bovine serum (FBS) and 1% penicillin-streptomycin.
- Phosphate-buffered saline (PBS), sterile.
- Trypsin-EDTA (0.25%), pre-warmed to 37°C.
- T-75 or T-175 tissue culture flasks.
- CO2 incubator set to 37°C, 5% CO2, 95% humidity.
- Laminar flow hood (biosafety cabinet).
- Inverted microscope.

## 3. Protocol

### 3.1 Thawing Cells

1. Remove cryovial from liquid nitrogen storage and place in 37°C water bath.
2. Gently swirl until a small ice crystal remains (approximately 1-2 minutes).
3. Wipe vial with 70% ethanol and transfer to biosafety cabinet.
4. Transfer cell suspension to a 15 mL conical tube containing 9 mL of pre-warmed complete medium.
5. Centrifuge at 300 × g for 5 minutes at room temperature.
6. Aspirate supernatant and resuspend pellet in 10 mL fresh complete medium.
7. Transfer to a T-75 flask and place in CO2 incubator.
8. Replace medium after 24 hours to remove residual DMSO.

### 3.2 Subculturing (Passaging)

Subculture when cells reach 80-90% confluence, approximately every 3-4 days.

1. Aspirate spent medium from the flask.
2. Wash cells with 5 mL PBS (T-75) or 10 mL PBS (T-175).
3. Add 2 mL (T-75) or 4 mL (T-175) of pre-warmed trypsin-EDTA.
4. Incubate at 37°C for 2-5 minutes until cells detach.
5. Add 8 mL (T-75) or 16 mL (T-175) of complete medium to neutralize trypsin.
6. Transfer cell suspension to a conical tube and centrifuge at 300 × g for 5 minutes.
7. Resuspend pellet in fresh complete medium.
8. Seed cells at a split ratio of 1:4 to 1:8, depending on cell type and growth rate.

### 3.3 Cryopreservation

1. Prepare freezing medium: complete medium with 10% DMSO and 20% FBS.
2. Resuspend cell pellet at approximately 2-6 × 10^6 cells/mL.
3. Dispense 1 mL aliquots into cryovials.
4. Place in controlled-rate freezing container at -80°C overnight.
5. Transfer to liquid nitrogen for long-term storage.

## 4. Quality Control

- Check cell morphology daily under inverted microscope.
- Test for mycoplasma contamination monthly.
- Record passage number for all cultures.
- Discard cells after passage 30 for HEK293 and passage 20 for HeLa.

## 5. Waste Disposal

All liquid waste containing live cells must be treated with 10% bleach for at least 30 minutes before disposal. Solid waste must be autoclaved.
